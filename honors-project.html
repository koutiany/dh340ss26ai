Honors Project

Chatbots from Major News Publishers

About the Project

This honors project explores the chatbots created by three major news publishers, examining how AI-powered conversational interfaces are being integrated into journalism and news delivery.

Research Focus

Project Overview

The student investigated how different news organizations have developed and deployed AI chatbots for various purposes, including:

  • News delivery and summarization
  • Reader engagement and interaction
  • Content recommendation
  • Fact-checking assistance

News Publishers Studied

Ask the Post AI

Analysis of their chatbot's approach to news delivery and interaction patterns.

Ask FT

Examination of how AI is used to enhance reader engagement and content discovery.

Atlantic Companion

Investigation into their implementation and use cases for AI-powered conversations.

Business Insider

Investigation into their implementation and use cases for AI-powered conversations.

Key Findings

Emerging Trends

The project reveals how news organizations are experimenting with AI to remain competitive and meet changing reader expectations in the digital age.

Implications for Digital Humanities

This research contributes to understanding how AI is reshaping media and communication, a central concern for digital humanities scholarship.

This honors project was completed as part of the DH340 course requirements.
