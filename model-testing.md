<section class="section">
    <h2>What We Tested</h2>
    <p>
        Students ran identical prompts across different models to observe how responses varied 
        based on model size, training data, and organizational origin.
    </p>
</section>

<section class="section">
    <h2>Model Origins & Organizations</h2>
    
    <div class="content-card">
        <h3>Meta (Facebook)</h3>
        <p>
            Meta has released several open-source models including the LLaMA series. Their models 
            are known for efficient performance relative to their size and have a strong research 
            focus.
        </p>
    </div>

    <div class="content-card">
        <h3>Mistral AI</h3>
        <p>
            A European AI company that has gained recognition for producing capable models with 
            efficient architectures. Their models often outperform larger models on various tasks.
        </p>
    </div>

    <div class="content-card">
        <h3>Microsoft</h3>
        <p>
            Through partnerships and research, Microsoft has contributed significantly to open-source 
            AI development, including Phi models optimized for specific use cases.
        </p>
    </div>

    <div class="content-card">
        <h3>Qwen (Alibaba)</h3>
        <p>
            Chinese tech giant Alibaba's Qwen models represent the growing global landscape of 
            AI development, bringing multilingual capabilities and diverse training approaches.
        </p>
    </div>

    <div class="content-card">
        <h3>DeepSeek</h3>
        <p>
            Another Chinese AI organization that has released open-source models, contributing to 
            the diversity of available options for research and experimentation.
        </p>
    </div>

    <div class="content-card">
        <h3>Other Open-Source Contributors</h3>
        <p>
            The open-source AI community includes numerous organizations and research groups 
            contributing models of varying sizes and specializations.
        </p>
    </div>
</section>

<section class="section">
    <h2>Key Observations</h2>
    <div class="highlight-box">
        <h3>Model Size Matters</h3>
        <p>
            Larger models generally produce more detailed and nuanced responses, but they also 
            require more computational resources. Students observed trade-offs between quality 
            and efficiency.
        </p>
    </div>
    <div class="highlight-box">
        <h3>Training Data Influence</h3>
        <p>
            Models trained on different datasets produce different styles and sometimes different 
            factual answers. This demonstrates why understanding model provenance is important.
        </p>
    </div>
    <div class="highlight-box">
        <h3>No Single "Best" Model</h3>
        <p>
            Different models excel at different tasks. The experience taught students to choose 
            tools based on specific needs rather than assuming one model fits all purposes.
        </p>
    </div>
</section>
