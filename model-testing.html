Model Testing & Comparison

Exploring open-source language models with LM Studio

Hands-on with Language Models

Using MSU's High Performance Computing Center's LM Studio, students experimented with various open-source language models. This hands-on experience helped demystify how AI systems actually work by comparing their outputs side-by-side.

What We Tested

Students ran identical prompts across different models to observe how responses varied based on model size,training data, and organizational origin.

Model Origins & Organizations

Meta (Facebook)

Meta has released several open-source models including the LLaMA series. Their models are known for efficient performance relative to their size and have a strong research focus.

Mistral AI

A European AI company that has gained recognition for producing capable models with efficient architectures. Their models often outperform larger models on various tasks.

Microsoft

Through partnerships and research, Microsoft has contributed significantly to open-source AI development, including Phi models optimized for specific use cases.

Qwen (Alibaba)

Chinese tech giant Alibaba's Qwen models represent the growing global landscape of AI development, bringing multilingual capabilities and diverse training approaches.

DeepSeek

Another Chinese AI organization that has released open-source models, contributing to the diversity of available options for research and experimentation.

Other Open-Source Contributors

The open-source AI community includes numerous organizations and research groups contributing models of varying sizes and specializations.

Key Observations

Model Size Matters

Larger models generally produce more detailed and nuanced responses, but they also require more computational resources. Students observed trade-offs between quality and efficiency.

Training Data Influence

Models trained on different datasets produce different styles and sometimes different factual answers. This demonstrates why understanding model provenance is important.

No Single "Best" Model

Different models excel at different tasks. The experience taught students to choose tools based on specific needs rather than assuming one model fits all purposes.

Questions for Critical Thinking

  • How does model ownership affect potential biases in responses?
  • What ethical considerations arise from using models developed by different organizations?
  • How might regulations in different countries affect AI development?
  • What does "open-source" really mean in the context of AI?
